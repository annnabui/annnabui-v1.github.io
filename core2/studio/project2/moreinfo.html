THE ELASTIC
BACK ROOM
how Instagram consumer profile-d me through 2 weeks of saved posts

a commentary on big data and media consumption

ABOUT:
☆ The Elastic Back Room project explores how social media algorithm connects advertisers to their appropriate target audience based on user activity. I chose Instagramas I am most active on this app and specifically focus on data from my Saved Posts because there is a higher chance of me revisiting these Posts in the future than Liked posts, hence more valuable to advertisers. I then ran these data through ChatGPT. ChatGPT is an excellent example of a modern algorithm because it is the most advanced among all the AI tools I can access. I initially considered comparing the analyses between different algorithms/AI bots but quickly found that most existing algorithms use OpenAI’s ChatGPT-3 language model. Data were only collected from 1 - 25 March to produce a more coherent analysis as ChatGPT has a limit to how much information it could go through at once.

Viewers can go through my Saved Posts chronologically or view them by Topics. Topics are tags that I actively follow. Under each topic, viewers can see corresponding posts as well as the kind of ad Instagram thought I would be interested in and have the opportunity to compare that with the type of ad ChatGPT figured I would be interested in under my “Account Information” section (accessed via the IG handle on my front page – “@hii_nnanaa”). They could also read ChatGPT’s logical reasoning behind each suggestion, which aims to mirror how Instagram’s algorithm works.

You can read my evaluation of these analyses in “Account Information.”
