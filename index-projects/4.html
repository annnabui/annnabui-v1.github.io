Parsons x IBM Quantum Design Jam: Dice or Die
Where a human protagonist tries to defeat otherworldly monsters in a dice match. A project aimed to explore the possibilities of replacing current gacha mechanics in probability-based games with quantum computing technology. Exhibited in Microscope Gallery, NYC. Collaborated with SB Park, Christine Chang & Arshya Gupta.

I was in charge of Art Direction for the game.

Overall Vision & Palette
I went with the 8-bit aesthetic to iterate this project as one of the first applications of quantum computing in the arts since pixels are commonly associated with early computer graphics.

Characters' color palette is generally cool-toned to contrast with the backgrounds' overall warm palette.
1. Background: Pre-battle
Warm & peaceful with light shades of pink and warm brown.
2. Background: Battle
Darker, more violent with purple, dark green, and cool earth tones.
3. Background: Halloween Edition
We decided to introduce a special skin since the game was released near Halloween. The color palette for this edition was dark red to reference traditional Halloween decorations.
4. Character Design – Protagonist
Meant to represent ‘peace’ – cool, natural color palette. Design is inclusive and gender neutral.
5. Character Design – Monster
Meant to look as if came from nature but its darker, twisted side.
6. Controller
The original controller wasn’t decorated, but we made a pumpkin controller upon release of the Halloween Edition. This controlled was later made the official controller for the game.
