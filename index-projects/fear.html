The Architecture of Fear
A Swiss-style editorial publishing on how fear influenced elements of architecture. Texts used are self-curated articles/publications from JSTOR and ProQuest.
< Main
The book is divided into three main parts:

1. Fearing the Unknown
––– Exploring the way in which physical spaces enhances the power imbalance between social groups, focusing on how men dominate space and how this dominance can be broken.
2. Fearing the Unseen
––– Investigating fully rendered illustrations of architecture that include the non-visible element of emotion that is essential in the becoming of architecture and place in film production.
3. Fearing the Unjust
––– A social-ecological examination of the correlates of residents’ fear of crime in new suburban housing developments.
